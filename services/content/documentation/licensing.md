# Licensing

**TL;DR:** systemprompt.io uses a dual-license model. The core platform is licensed under the Business Source License 1.1 (BSL-1.1), which automatically converts to Apache 2.0 after four years. The template layer is MIT-licensed. **Production use of the core platform requires a negotiated licence agreement** — it is not automatically permitted under the BSL-1.1.

## License Model

systemprompt.io ships under two licenses that work together:

| Component | License | What It Means |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Core platform** | Business Source License 1.1 (BSL-1.1) | Source-available. Production use requires a negotiated licence. Converts to Apache 2.0 after four years. |
| **Template layer** | MIT | Fully open. Use it however you want — commercial, private, modified, or unmodified. |

## BSL-1.1 Terms

The Business Source License governs the systemprompt.io core:

| Term | Details |
|------|---------|
| **License type** | Business Source License 1.1 |
| **Production use** | **Requires a negotiated licence agreement.** Not automatically permitted. |
| **Evaluation & development** | Permitted for non-production evaluation, testing, and development purposes |
| **Change date** | Four years from each version's release |
| **Conversion license** | Apache License, Version 2.0 |

After the change date, each version's license automatically converts to Apache 2.0 with no restrictions and no action required on your part.

## What You Can Do

### Without a Production Licence

You are free to:

- **Evaluate and develop** — run systemprompt.io in non-production environments for evaluation, testing, and development
- **Access full source code** — read, audit, learn from, and extend the codebase
- **Modify the template** — the MIT-licensed template is yours to customise without restriction

### With a Negotiated Production Licence

Once a licence agreement is in place, you can:

- **Deploy in production** — run systemprompt.io in your own production environments
- **Build proprietary products** — create commercial products and services on top of the platform
- **Self-host** — run on your own infrastructure with full control

## Restrictions

The BSL-1.1 license prohibits three things:

| Restriction | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **No competing service** | You may not offer systemprompt.io itself as a hosted service that competes with the official platform |
| **No reselling core hosting** | You may not resell hosting of the core platform to third parties |
| **License notices** | You may not remove license notices from the core codebase |

These restrictions exist to protect the sustainability of the project. They do not restrict building SaaS products or commercial applications _on top of_ the platform.

## Code Ownership

The template layer is MIT-licensed — it belongs to you. Your customizations, configurations, plugins, skills, agents, and extensions are your intellectual property. They never leave your infrastructure unless you choose to share them.

There is no requirement to open-source any code you build on the platform.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use systemprompt.io in production without a licence?**
No. Production use of the core platform requires a negotiated licence agreement. You can evaluate, test, and develop against the platform freely, but deploying to production requires a licence.

**Can I build a SaaS product using systemprompt.io?**
Yes, with a production licence in place. You can build and sell commercial products on the platform. The restriction only applies to offering systemprompt.io itself as a competing hosted service.

**Do I need to open-source my plugins or customisations?**
No. The template is MIT-licensed and your customisations are your own. There is no copyleft requirement.

**What happens after four years?**
Each version of the core automatically converts from BSL-1.1 to Apache 2.0. After conversion there are no restrictions at all.

**Can I self-host?**
Yes, with a self-hosted production licence. The platform is source-available and you can evaluate it on your own infrastructure freely. Production deployment requires a licence agreement.

**What if I need custom terms?**
Contact us about enterprise licensing. We offer custom arrangements including sandboxed builds with perpetual licences, tailored to your organisation's requirements.
