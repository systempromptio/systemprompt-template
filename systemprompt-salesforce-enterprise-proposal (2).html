"Why not just use Salesforce Agentforce?"

Agentforce runs inside Salesforce. We govern AI across it.

Agentforce is a strong fit for agents that live entirely within the Salesforce tenant. The enterprise reality is that AI has to act across Salesforce and everything around it — and your security team needs one audit trail and one policy plane over all of it. That is a different job, and a complementary one.

Cross-system, not tenant-locked

Agentforce governs actions inside the Salesforce tenant. systemprompt.io governs tool calls across Salesforce, your other systems, files and custom MCP servers — one policy plane over the whole estate, not one per app.

Multi-model, your provider

Route to Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, Groq or a self-hosted Llama/Qwen per department, task or cost rule — including zero-marginal-cost on-prem inference. You are not bound to one vendor's model roadmap or list price.

Client-owned audit trail

Every call lands in audit_events in your PostgreSQL, linked by trace_id to a verified identity, exportable to your SIEM. The compliance boundary is yours end-to-end — not held inside a vendor tenant.

Why this passes audit

Govern every tool call. Prove every decision.

When the auditor asks what AI did and who authorised it, you run one query. systemprompt.io is a single self-hosted Rust binary that authenticates, authorises, rate-limits, logs and costs every AI interaction — before it ever reaches your data. PostgreSQL is the only dependency.

Governance pipeline

Four sub-millisecond Rust checks on every tool call at the handler boundary: permission (RBAC), secret scan, blocklist, rate limit. Role, department and per-entity rules decide what passes. A denied call never reaches the model.

scope · secret · blocklist · rate

Secrets never touch inference

Credentials flow through MCP services, not inference endpoints. The agent calls the tool; the MCP service injects the credential server-side. No secrets in context windows, no secrets in logs, no leakage via prompt injection.

server-side injection

One audit_events row per AI request, tool call and MCP execution — written before the response returns. Full lineage from request → tool → MCP → cost, linked by trace_id to a JWT-verified identity.

What a "skill" is — the unit you license

A skill is a catalogued, version-controlled, tested workflow — not an open-ended prompt. Each ships as portable YAML + Markdown with a defined trigger, scoped tool permissions (which MCP servers and Salesforce objects it may touch), validation against your real data, a usage guide, and a semantic version. Skills live in your central registry, are signed and distributed per role and department, and are billed as discrete catalog items with a fixed build cost and a known delivery timeline. Buyers expand by adding catalogued skills — not by funding vague "agentic capability."
